## ⛔ THE OPERATING PROTOCOL (mandatory, every time)

Before ANY task involving marketing, creative, or strategy — scripts, hooks, ads, VSLs, landing pages, emails, angles, offers, naming, campaign structure, budgets, testing, analysis, funnel decisions, or strategic recommendations:

1. **Read this file top to bottom.**
2. **Read the project's `PROJECT_CONTEXT.md`** (the brand's offer, audience, funnel, voice, constraints).
3. **Use the Master Trigger Map below** to identify which source file(s) govern the task.
4. **Read the governing file(s) in `BRAIN/` in full** — not from memory of past sessions.
5. **Ground the work at SECTION level, not book level.** Every significant recommendation must cite the specific framework/section/rule it rests on (e.g., "Schwartz §4, Stage-3 sophistication → lead with the mechanism," "Hopkins: cost per [conversion event] is the only verdict," "Meta masterclass: frequency ≤2 health check"), never a vague "per Schwartz." If sources tension on the point, name the tension and apply the resolution logic in the Synthesis below. If a decision contradicts the Brain, state the contradiction and the reason explicitly.
6. **Escalate to the raw text when the decision is high-stakes or the distillation feels thin.** Full source texts live in `sources/` (books as .txt, videos as transcripts). For major calls — choosing/killing an angle, restructuring a funnel, big budget moves — grep the relevant raw text for the governing passage and quote it. The distillations are the studied working memory; the raw texts are the library shelf. Both are part of the Brain.

## MASTER TRIGGER MAP (task → source)

- **Pick or fix an angle / "why isn't this ad working?"** → 01 Schwartz (awareness state + sophistication diagnosis) → then 05 (statistical reality: ~5% creative hit rate; iterate hooks before killing angles).
- **Write or review a hook** → 01 (awareness-matched headline job) + 03a (4 U's, 8 types, 6–8 versions minimum) + 05 (problem agitation > persona callout; hook = 80% of watch decision) + 06 (90% of creative time on hook; verbatim customer language).
- **Write or review a script / VSL / long-form ad** → 03b (full VSL spec) + 01 (gradualization, intensification, complete-ad architecture) + the brand's voice rules (in `PROJECT_CONTEXT.md`).
- **Landing page** → 03b (conversion frame) + 04 (natural reading sequence; "fat in this dress").
- **Offer / pricing / guarantee / lead magnet / trial** → 02 (samples, offers, specificity) + 01 (guarantee as climax — Intensification Technique 13) + 06 (LTV:CAC floors; ascension back-end).
- **Campaign structure / budget / testing plan / kill-or-scale decisions** → 05 (the governing system) + 02 (test-campaign law; cost per final conversion event, never vanity metrics).
- **Analyzing results** → 05 (judge at ad-set level, 7-day click; amount-spent signal; frequency ≤2) + 02 (cost-per-customer is final).
- **Brand/positioning/naming/new product or upsell** → 04 (focus test, extension rules, 7 naming criteria) + 06 (contrast, purple ocean).
- **Email/follow-up sequences** → 03b (3–7 email conversion series).
- **Anything economics** → 06 (3:1 LTV:CAC floor, 7–10:1 high-ticket target) + 02.
- **"Is this idea good?" general sanity check** → 06 + 04 (zig/zag; "scares the hell out of everybody").

## CROSS-SOURCE SYNTHESIS — the one decision process

The six sources are one machine when run in this order:

1. **Neumeier defines the brand**: who are you / what do you do / why does it matter — and the answers must be different, focused, and *behaved*, not just claimed. Every downstream choice must not blur them.
2. **Schwartz chooses the battlefield**: identify the mass desire already in the prospect's head (you channel demand, you never create it), diagnose the prospect's **awareness state** per angle and the market's **sophistication stage**, and let those dictate the hook's job. Watch for the sub-market reset: a genuinely new sub-market inside a "saturated" category re-runs the sophistication cycle from Stage 1 — plain direct claims work again there.
3. **Bly writes it**: the motivating sequence for structure, the 4 U's for every hook (write 6–8 versions, keep the best), the VSL spec for long-form, BDF (beliefs/desires/feelings — especially the private feelings the prospect won't say out loud) for emotional targeting, readability rules always.
4. **Hopkins disciplines the claims and the test**: specifics over superlatives (a specific claim is accepted at face value; a superlative discounts everything around it), positive outpulls negative ~4:1, prevention doesn't sell (sell the cure/gain), samples/trials follow the sampling laws, and **the only verdict that counts is cost per final conversion event**.
5. **The Meta masterclass runs the account** (for Meta/paid-social channels): creative IS the targeting (one concept = persona+angle+offer per ad set; never mix); never judge single ads on last-click ROAS (sequencing: openers feed closers); amount-spent = the performance signal; frequency ≤2 on cold; ~5% creative hit rate (zero winners from a first batch is *normal* — iterate hooks, which are nearly free, before touching bodies or killing angles); introduce the product late; pre-screen hooks free with CTA-less organic posts on a founder/creator profile.
6. **The 8-years operator audits everything**: does this look *nothing* like what every competitor runs (contrast is the master variable)? Is unit economics above floor (LTV:CAC ≥3:1, 7–10:1 for high-ticket — and if front-end is thin, where's the ascension back-end)? Are we spending 90% of creative time on the hook, in verbatim customer language? Did we try to break the funnel before launch?

### Where sources tension — and the resolutions
- **Audience callouts vs problem agitation:** naming the audience ("Nurses, read this") is proven in some markets, but the Meta masterclass shows *generic* callouts get skipped and problem agitation targets better. Resolution: agitate an **audience-specific pain** so precisely the callout is implicit; if you use a literal callout, make it carry information, not just address.
- **Hopkins' "positive outpulls negative 4:1" vs pain-led hooks (Bly's BDF, the masterclass's agitation):** open on the *specific* pain for recognition, resolve fast into the positive picture. Never dwell in the pain; and never sell prevention-of-decline about the prospect *himself* (Schwartz: prevention is only visualized for loved ones/others — displace decline onto third parties).
- **Schwartz's "your most powerful claim does not always make your most powerful headline" vs bold-claim hooks:** at Stage 3–4 sophistication, prepare belief before claiming (mechanism first, gradualization); at Stage 1–2 (fresh markets or sub-market resets), the direct claim is still fresh and works. Diagnose the stage before choosing the hook type.
- **Neumeier's "zig when others zag" vs Hopkins' "don't educate the market":** contrast in *presentation and angle* is cheap and powerful; creating a brand-new category is expensive (Hopkins' tested example: toothbrush-habit converts cost $20–25 each, and competitors share the harvest). Zig inside proven demand — the purple-ocean play (proven market, hyper-specific segment you own 100%).
